Common Questions

Everything you need to know.

How much does drone spraying cost per acre?
Our Starter plan begins at LKR 8,500 per acre for spraying only, while the Professional plan including NDVI mapping starts at LKR 12,000 per acre. Enterprise seasonal contracts are priced based on total acreage, frequency, and district. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your exact farm.
What is the minimum farm size you service?
We service farms from 1 acre upward. While larger farms gain the most from drone efficiency, even 1–3 acre plots benefit significantly in speed, precision, and reduced chemical usage compared to manual spraying.
Is drone spraying safe for my crops and the environment?
Absolutely. The DJI Agras T25 uses precision atomization nozzles that deliver fine droplets directly onto crop surfaces, minimising chemical drift and runoff. It uses up to 90% less water and 30% fewer chemicals than traditional spraying, making it significantly safer for your crops, soil microbiome, and surrounding waterways.
How soon can you deploy after I book?
For Standard plan clients, scheduling within 48 hours. For Professional plan clients, we guarantee 24-hour priority scheduling. Urgent emergency requests may be accommodated same-day — call our hotline directly for fastest response.
What happens if weather conditions are bad on the mission day?
We monitor meteorological data 48 hours in advance. Strong winds above 10 m/s, heavy rain, or visibility below operational minimums will trigger an automatic reschedule at no additional cost. Weather holds are communicated to you as early as possible via phone call.
What does the NDVI mapping report show me?
NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) mapping uses multispectral cameras to assess your crop's photosynthetic activity. The report shows a colour-coded map of your field highlighting healthy areas (dark green), stressed areas (yellow-orange), and severely affected zones (red). This enables targeted intervention before visible symptoms appear.
Do you operate outside the five listed districts?
Our current active zones are Anuradhapura, Kurunegala, Ampara, Polonnaruwa, and Kilinochchi. We are actively expanding in 2025. Special coverage in adjacent districts may be arranged for large-area Enterprise clients. Contact us to discuss your specific location.
What crops can the drone spray?
Our drones service paddy/rice, maize, vegetables, coconut, rubber, sugarcane, tea, banana, chili, and many others. Mission parameters including altitude, spray rate, droplet size, and flight speed are calibrated per crop type based on DJI's crop-specific protocols for optimal results.
